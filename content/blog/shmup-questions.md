I've recently been working on a jam-sized [SHMUP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shoot_%27em_up). I can't tell you exactly why, but I *can* tell you that I'm woefully out of my depth! I wasn't particullarly familiar with the genre before starting the project, and there is a lot more to it than I was expecting! During my research, I've uncovered a wealth of information on what makes a SHMUP "tick". While I did find some content from SHMUP developers, the most useful resource was from players. Tutorials, reviews, and testamonials -from the people who actualy enjoy the genra. I learned about "popcorn" enemies, economy of movement, leading bullets, and generally what makes a SHMUP fun to play.

Here is a non-exhaustive list of questions I had to answer about my own game:

- Should levels be random, or pre-defined?
- Should be it be one long stage, or different playable levels?
- How fast should the player move?
- How long should a stage be?
- How long before the first boss/miniboss?
- Should the screen ever be empty, or always have enemies on it?
- Is the "point" to survive, or get a high score?
- Should there be screen shake?
- Should movement be on two axis, or one axis?
- Should an enemy eventually leave the screen if not killed?
- Should there be visible score/damage numbers?
- Should there be healthbars? 
- How to seperate bullets and enemies from the background, without making the background boring?
- How small should the players hitbox be?
- Should bullet hitbox match their visuals? 
- Should enemies have contact damage?
